A Travel Journal — Assam, India

Where the
Brahmaputra
Writes the Map

From the tea gardens of Jorhat to the rhinos of Kaziranga, the silk looms of Sualkuchi to the Vaishnavite Satras of Majuli — this is a hand-curated guide to the Assam most itineraries skip past.

Where to Go

Six places worth the detour
Wildlife

Kaziranga National Park

One-horned rhinos, elephant-back safaris through tall grass, and the Brahmaputra's floodplain ecosystem at its most alive. Best visited November to April.

Faith & River

Majuli Island

The world's largest river island, home to centuries-old Vaishnavite Satras — monastic centres of Assamese art, dance, and mask-making. Reachable by ferry from Jorhat.

Craft

Sualkuchi — Silk Village

The heart of Muga and Pat silk weaving. Walk through homes where looms run in every courtyard, producing silk once reserved for Ahom royalty.

History

Charaideo — Ahom Maidams

Royal burial mounds of the Ahom dynasty, often called the "Pyramids of Assam." Quiet, underrated, and steeped in 600 years of history.

Tea

Jorhat Tea Estates

Walk the gardens that supply tea to the world, stay in colonial-era planter bungalows, and learn the difference between orthodox and CTC processing.

City & River

Guwahati

Gateway to Northeast India. Kamakhya Temple on Nilachal Hill, sunset cruises on the Brahmaputra, and the best base for day trips across Lower Assam.

Culture, Not Just Scenery

Four threads running through every region

Bihu

Assam's harvest festival, marked three times a year — each tied to the agricultural calendar and celebrated with dance, song, and feasting.

Gamosa

A handwoven cotton cloth with red woven motifs — used in welcome, worship, and daily life. A small object that carries large meaning.

Xorai

A bell-metal offering tray, traditionally used to honour guests — its concentric form recurs throughout Assamese ceremonial design.

Vaishnavite Heritage

The Satra institutions founded by Srimanta Sankardeva shaped Assamese art, music, and theatre — and remain living centres today.

When to Go

A rough month-by-month read
Jan–Feb

Peak Season

Cool, dry. Best for Kaziranga safaris and the Magh Bihu harvest season.

Mar–Apr

Bohag Bihu

Assamese New Year — the most vibrant cultural window of the year.

May–Sep

Monsoon

Heavy rain, river levels rise dramatically. Majuli ferries may be affected — check ahead.

Oct–Nov

Post-Monsoon

Landscapes are at their greenest. Durga Puja season in Guwahati.

Dec

Tea & Cool Air

Comfortable temperatures, ideal for tea estate visits in Jorhat.
